# Sentence 1:

**Affordable early years education and childcare potentially enables parents, particularly mothers, to be in paid employment.**

Affordable early years education and childcare potentially enables parents, particularly mothers, to be in paid employment.

**Easy Explanation:**

When childcare and early education are not expensive, parents — especially mothers — can go to work and earn money.

Here, the idea is simple: cheaper childcare → parents can work.

# Sentence 2:

**International studies a)_____ that countries with greater enrolment rates in publicly funded or provided childcare also have higher maternal employment rates, although untangling causal relationships is complex.**

---

## 1) Easy explanation

**Original Line:**

International studies have found that countries with greater enrolment rates in publicly funded or provided childcare also have higher maternal employment rates, although untangling causal relationships is complex.

**Easy Explanation:**

Research from different countries has discovered that when more children attend government-funded childcare, more mothers are employed. However, it is difficult to clearly prove the exact cause-and-effect relationship.

Main idea: More childcare enrolment → higher employment of mothers.

But: Cause and effect is hard to prove.

---

## 2) SVO Breakdown

**Main Clause:**

- Subject: International studies
- Verb: have found
- Object: that countries … have higher maternal employment rates

**Noun Clause (object of "have found"):** "that countries with greater enrolment rates in publicly funded or provided childcare also have higher maternal employment rates"

- Subject: countries
- Verb: have
- Object: higher maternal employment rates

**Modifiers:**

- with greater enrolment rates (prepositional phrase modifying "countries")
- in publicly funded or provided childcare (modifies enrolment rates)
- also (adverb – addition)

**Subordinate Clause (concession clause):** "although untangling causal relationships is complex"

- Subject: untangling causal relationships
- Verb: is
- Complement: complex
- Type: Concessive clause (shows contrast).

---

## 3) Grammar rule or collocation applied to choose the option for sentence 2

### Blank (a)

**Correct answer: have found**

**Why?**

Subject = **International studies** (plural)

So verb must agree → **have**, not has.

Now check options:

1. **have found** — Correct tense (present perfect), active voice. Collocation: *studies have found that…* (very common in academic writing).
2. **have been found** — Passive voice. Structure would be wrong: "International studies have been found that…" — This makes no sense because *studies* cannot be "found that".
3. **found** — Simple past. Grammatically possible, but in academic writing we usually use **present perfect** to describe research findings that are still relevant. "Studies have found" = results are still true now.
4. **have yet to find** — Meaning: they have not found. This completely changes the meaning.

**Rule:** Use **present perfect (have found)** for general research findings that are still valid.

---

## 4) Mistake analysis

You chose **have found** — Excellent. This shows good understanding of subject-verb agreement and academic tense usage.

# Sentence 3:

**From the point of view of the household, additional income, especially for the less well-off, is itself associated with better outcomes for children, as child poverty b)____ to be a key independent determinant of children's outcomes.**

---

## 1) Easy explanation

**Original Line:**

From the point of view of the household, additional income… is associated with better outcomes for children, as child poverty has been shown to be a key independent determinant of children's outcomes.

**Easy Explanation:**

From a family's perspective, earning more money leads to better results for children. This is because research has shown that child poverty strongly affects children's future outcomes.

---

## 2) SVO Breakdown

**Main Clause:**

- Subject: additional income
- Verb: is
- Complement: associated with better outcomes

**Modifiers:**

- From the point of view of the household
- especially for the less well-off

**Subordinate Clause (reason clause – "as"):** "as child poverty has been shown to be a key independent determinant of children's outcomes"

- Subject: child poverty
- Verb: has been shown (passive, present perfect)
- Complement: to be a key independent determinant

**Infinitive Clause:** "to be a key independent determinant of children's outcomes"

- Subject: child poverty (understood)
- Verb: be
- Complement: determinant

---

## 3) Grammar rule or collocation applied to choose the option

### Blank (b)

**Correct answer: has been shown**

**Why?**

Subject = **child poverty** (singular)

So we need singular verb → **has**

Check options:

1. **have shown** — Plural verb. Subject is singular. Also active voice would require object: "child poverty have shown…"
2. **has been shown** — Correct tense (present perfect), passive voice. Collocation: *has been shown to be…* (very common academic structure).
3. **was showing** — Past continuous. Completely wrong meaning and tense.
4. **showed** — Simple past. Possible grammatically, but academic writing prefers present perfect passive to show general established research.

**Rule:** Academic writing often uses **present perfect passive** to describe established research findings.

# Sentence 4:

**From the point of view of the public purse, as mothers c)______ employment, they are likely to claim fewer benefits and to generate extra revenues d)____ income tax and national insurance contributions.**

---

## 1) Easy explanation

**Original Line:**

From the point of view of the public purse, as mothers enter employment, they are likely to claim fewer benefits and to generate extra revenues through income tax and national insurance contributions.

**Easy Explanation:**

From the government's perspective, when mothers start working, they receive fewer government benefits and pay more taxes.

---

## 2) SVO Breakdown

**Subordinate Clause (time clause – "as"):** "as mothers enter employment"

- Subject: mothers
- Verb: enter
- Object: employment

**Main Clause:**

- Subject: they
- Verb: are likely
- Complement: to claim fewer benefits and to generate extra revenues

**Infinitive 1:** to claim fewer benefits

**Infinitive 2:** to generate extra revenues

Modifier for generate:

- through income tax and national insurance contributions

---

## 3) Grammar rule or collocation applied to choose the option

### Blank (c)

**Correct answer: enter**

**Why?**

Structure: as + subject + verb

We need a finite verb (present simple).

Check options:

1. enact — Wrong meaning. Enact = officially make a law.
2. enacting — Gerund/participle. Cannot use after "as" without auxiliary verb.
3. **enter** — Correct meaning and correct grammar (present simple). Collocation: *enter employment* (very common).
4. entering — Participle. Would need structure like: "As mothers are entering…"

**Rule:** After conjunction "as" (meaning when), use **subject + base verb (present simple)**.

---

### Blank (d)

**Correct answer: through**

**Why?**

Collocation: generate revenue **through** tax

Check options:

1. **through** — Means "by means of".
2. within — Means inside.
3. by — Possible sometimes, but "generate revenue by income tax" sounds unnatural. We say "through".
4. throughout — Means in every part of.

**Rule:** Correct preposition collocation = **revenue through tax**.

---

## 4) Mistake analysis

You wrote:

- a) have found
- b) has been shown
- c) entering
- d) through

Your only mistake = **c**

**Why did you choose "entering"?**

Probably you thought: "as mothers entering employment…"

But grammar rule: After subordinating conjunction "as" → need full clause (subject + verb).

"Entering" is not a complete verb. It is a participle.

To use entering, sentence would need: "As mothers are entering employment…"

So this is a **finite vs non-finite verb confusion**.

For 79+ score, you must instantly check: After conjunction → full clause required.
