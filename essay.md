# PTE Essay

**Prompt:** What are the problems and the benefits for high school students of studying plays and other works for theatre that were written centuries ago? Do you agree with it? Use your own experience to discuss it.

**Type:** Double question (mixed) — problems/benefits; agreement + experience.

---

High school courses often include plays and theatre works from past centuries. Some see these works as bridges to culture and language skills. Others see them as slow texts with hard wording for busy teenagers. This essay will discuss the problems and the benefits of studying theatre works written centuries ago, and whether I agree, using my own experience and a pragmatic approach.

Classic plays from past centuries mix poetry and drama in long stage texts. They benefit students because they build rich vocabulary and show society in past times. The main problems are heavy language and slow scenes, and students may lose focus during class. For example, my class read a long play, but the teacher gave summaries before each act, and as a result I followed the plot and felt the emotion. Therefore, studying old theatre works still has a positive impact in these key areas.

I agree with keeping these works because skills outweigh stress for most students. Although the language is old, the texts still teach empathy and family conflict. The problems come from hard words, not from the lack of value in the stories. For example, I joined a class performance, and I felt shy at first, but I gained confidence; as a result, some still feel stress, but these problems stem from hard language, not from plays, and when teachers set clear steps, students can avoid them.

In conclusion, old theatre works bring benefits in culture and language, but they have also raised concerns about hard language. My conviction is clear: the case for benefit is stronger than the case for harm, and with sensible teaching it remains valuable.
