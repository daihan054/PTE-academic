**Prompt:** What are the problems and the benefits for high school students of studying plays and other works for theatre that were written centuries ago? Do you agree with it? Use your own experience to discuss it.

Old plays appear in class daily. Some believe studying these works has a beneficial impact. Others argue it has a detrimental impact. This essay will discuss problems and benefits of studying theatre works written centuries ago and determine whether its impact is beneficial or detrimental, using a pragmatic approach.

The centuries-old play combines drama and poetry in one tool. It has changed how we communicate, work, and access information, and is among the most influential. It benefits society because it gives people reliable access to vital services and information; people can call for help when in danger and students can learn when schools are closed. For example, during the COVID-19 pandemic, schools closed, but smartphones allowed children without computers to continue learning, and as a result millions kept learning. Therefore, old plays have a positive impact in these areas.

On the other hand, some argue that old plays damage health and social life. Excessive use is linked to sleep problems and less activity, and social media can increase stress. When people use phones too much, they spend less time face-to-face. For example, at family dinners people look at their phones instead of talking to each other, which weakens real connections; as a result, some experience worse well-being, but these drawbacks stem from use, not the device, and when people set limits, they can avoid them.

In conclusion, studying old plays brings benefits in communication, education, and emergency access, but has also raised concerns about screen time and well-being. My conviction is that the case for benefit is stronger than the case for harm, and with sensible use it remains valuable.
