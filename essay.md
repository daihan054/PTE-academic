# PTE Essay

**Prompt:** What are the problems and the benefits for high school students of studying plays and other works for theatre that were written centuries ago? Do you agree with it? Use your own experience to discuss it.

**Type:** Double question (mixed) — problems/benefits; agreement + experience.

---

High school students in many countries often read classic plays and theatre texts in literature classes. These old works can teach culture and strong language skills. They can often feel distant and hard to understand for teenagers. This essay will address both questions: the problems and benefits of studying old theatre works in high school, and whether I agree, using a pragmatic approach.

In light of the first question, classic theatre study offers both gains and challenges. First, students gain rich vocabulary and a clearer picture of history and human emotions on stage. Second, long scenes and old wording can confuse beginners, and weekly class time may feel heavy. When I read a centuries-old play in class, I struggled with the opening lines, but my teacher used simple summaries so that I could follow the plot. As a result, I saw the story still had real meaning for me today.

From my point of view, I agree with studying these works in high school because the long-term skills matter more than short-term confusion. Although the texts are old, they push students to think deeply about choices, power, and family ties. In my own experience, I joined a short class performance of a classic scene, and I felt shy at first but later proud. Therefore, I support keeping old theatre works in the curriculum when teachers give clear support.

In summary, old plays bring clear benefits and real problems for high school students, but guided study can reduce the hardest parts. My conviction rests on one idea: schools should keep these works and balance them with simple explanations and active tasks for teenagers.
